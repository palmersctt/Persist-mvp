# Persistwork Personas

## Primary: The Burnt-Out IC

**Who:** An individual contributor (engineer, designer, PM) buried in meetings.

- 6-9 meetings/day is normal. Some days hit double digits.
- Has maybe one 30-minute gap they call "lunch" but spend catching up on Slack.
- Knows their calendar is bad but can't articulate *why* it feels so draining.
- Context-switching all afternoon — sprint planning, design review, client sync, 1:1, all-hands — with zero recovery time between.
- Doesn't need another productivity tool telling them to "block focus time." They've tried. The meetings win.
- What they actually need: validation that the exhaustion is real, a number that makes it visible, and something to laugh about so they don't spiral.

**Mood on a typical day:** Bad tier (survival, grinding, scattered). Occasionally OK (autopilot). Rarely good.

**Key moment:** Opens Persistwork in the morning, sees their scores, reads the quote, and thinks "yeah, that's exactly right." The card names what they already feel.

**Share behavior:** Screenshots the card and sends it to a coworker or drops it in a team Slack channel. The joke is the entry point — the scores are the proof.

---

## Secondary: The Share Card Recipient

**Who:** A colleague, friend, or manager who receives a shared card.

- Doesn't use Persistwork (yet). Sees the card as an image or screenshot.
- First impression is the quote — funny, dark, relatable. That's the hook.
- Second thing they notice: the scores. "Wait, 81 strain? What does that mean?"
- Third: the mood label. "Survival Mode" — they get it immediately.
- Reaction is either "lol same" (fellow IC) or "oh... is your calendar really that bad?" (manager discovering the problem).

**What the card needs to do for them:**
1. Be immediately funny/relatable (quote does this)
2. Convey severity without explanation (scores + mood label do this)
3. Make them want to check their own calendar (conversion path)

**Key moment:** They see the card, laugh, then look at their own calendar and think "mine might be worse." That's when they sign up.
